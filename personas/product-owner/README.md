# FORGE Persona: Product Owner

## Who Is This?

Product Owners are the Scrum team's primary connection to the business. They own the product backlog, write and refine user stories, define acceptance criteria, and accept completed work on behalf of the stakeholder community. Unlike the Product Manager who operates at the strategic level, the Product Owner works at the execution level — translating strategy into actionable, sprint-ready stories that development teams can pick up and build.

In the context of enterprise modernization programs, Product Owners are critical translators between the business requirements captured by Business Analysts and the technical tasks defined by Lead Engineers. They ensure that what gets built matches what was asked for, and that the team has a clear, prioritized path forward at all times.

---

## What AI Can Help a Product Owner Do

FORGE accelerates the most time-consuming parts of a Product Owner's work:

- **Write well-formed user stories** with Given/When/Then acceptance criteria, edge cases, and non-functional requirements included from the start
- **Decompose large epics** into appropriately-sized, independent stories that a team can estimate and deliver in a sprint
- **Audit a backlog** for INVEST compliance, testability, and completeness — catching issues before refinement sessions
- **Generate Definition of Done** frameworks tailored to the team's context and delivery standards
- **Draft sprint goals** that are measurable and communicate value rather than just listing tasks
- **Create estimation reference cards** so the team has consistent, shared understanding of story point meaning
- **Identify story dependencies** and suggest sequencing to minimize blocking

## SDLC Stages This Persona Participates In

| Stage | Role |
|-------|------|
| **Discovery** | Consumer — receives PRD and business context from Product Manager |
| **Requirements** | Primary — writes and owns all user stories and acceptance criteria |
| **Sprint Planning** | Facilitator — presents and explains stories, ensures team understanding |
| **Build** | Reviewer — answers questions, clarifies acceptance criteria, unblocks team |
| **Sprint Review** | Acceptor — accepts or rejects completed stories against acceptance criteria |
| **UAT** | Coordinator — works with UAT team to validate stories against business intent |

## How to Use FORGE as a Product Owner

1. **Start with the PRD.** The best user stories come from a clear PRD. Before running the Epic Decomposition prompt, ensure you have the PRD from your Product Manager and any business rules from the Business Analyst.
2. **Run backlog refinement before every sprint.** Use the Backlog Refinement prompt to audit your story queue a week before refinement sessions. Fix issues before the team sees them.
3. **Use the Definition of Done as a baseline.** Generate it once per project, then keep it in your team's wiki. Reference it in every story's acceptance criteria section.
4. **Pair with the BA.** The Business Analyst's process maps and business rule extractions are your best input for edge cases. Use them when writing acceptance criteria.
