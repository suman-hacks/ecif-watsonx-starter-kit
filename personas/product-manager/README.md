# FORGE Persona: Product Manager

## Who Is This?

Product Managers own the product vision and strategy. They are responsible for defining what gets built, why it gets built, and how success is measured. They spend most of their time at the intersection of customer needs, business goals, and technical feasibility — synthesizing inputs from many sources into clear, actionable direction for engineering teams.

In the context of enterprise modernization and SDLC delivery, Product Managers are the authors of product strategy documents, the owners of roadmaps, and the primary communicators between engineering teams and executive stakeholders.

---

## What AI Can Help a Product Manager Do

AI assistance dramatically accelerates the PM's document-heavy, synthesis-heavy work. With FORGE, a Product Manager can:

- **Draft PRDs in minutes** rather than days, starting from discovery notes and business objectives
- **Generate OKR frameworks** that connect business strategy to measurable product outcomes
- **Run structured competitive analysis** without starting from a blank page
- **Write release notes** for multiple audiences simultaneously (technical, business, end-user)
- **Translate technical findings** (risk assessments, architecture reviews) into executive-ready language
- **Apply prioritization frameworks** (RICE, MoSCoW) to a backlog with AI-generated scoring guidance
- **Build ROI models** for modernization proposals and investment justifications

The AI acts as a senior document drafter — you provide the strategic thinking, context, and judgment; the AI structures it into professional, complete documents.

## How to Use FORGE as a Product Manager

1. **Start with context.** The better your inputs (discovery notes, business objectives, competitor names, raw data), the more useful the AI output. Before running a prompt, gather your notes.
2. **Use the PRD prompt as your anchor.** Most other PM outputs (OKRs, roadmaps, presentations) flow from a solid PRD. Run it first.
3. **Iterate, don't accept.** AI-generated documents are first drafts. Read them critically, add your strategic judgment, and refine before sharing.
4. **Chain to other personas.** Your PRD feeds the BA's Business Rule Extraction and the PO's Epic Decomposition. Your competitive analysis informs the Architect's design decisions.
